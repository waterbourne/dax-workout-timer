# North Indian Cuisine Foundation

## Regional Breakdown

### Punjab
- **Character**: Rich, buttery, hearty
- **Key ingredients**: Ghee, paneer, cream, mustard greens, wheat
- **Signature dishes**: Makki di roti + sarson ka saag, paneer tikka, dal makhani, chole bhature
- **Technique**: Slow-cooked lentils, tandoor cooking, generous use of dairy

### Rajasthan
- **Character**: Spicy, arid-climate adapted, preserved foods
- **Key ingredients**: Gram flour (besan), lentils, dried spices, buttermilk
- **Signature dishes**: Dal baati churma, gatte ki sabzi, ker sangri, laal maas
- **Technique**: Sun-drying, fermentation, minimal water cooking

### Kashmir
- **Character**: Aromatic, mild heat, yogurt-based
- **Key ingredients**: Fennel, ginger, saffron, yogurt, lamb
- **Signature dishes**: Rogan josh, yakhni, dum aloo, kashmiri pulao
- **Technique**: Dum pukht (slow steam), yogurt marinades, whole spice infusion

### Uttar Pradesh/Awadhi
- **Character**: Royal, nuanced, layered
- **Key ingredients**: Mutton, saffron, nuts, silver leaf, aromatic rice
- **Signature dishes**: Biryani, galouti kebab, korma, sheermal
- **Technique**: Dum cooking, minced meat expertise, refined spice blends

### Delhi
- **Character**: Street food hub, Mughlai influence
- **Key ingredients**: Same as above but with kababs, chaat, parathas
- **Signature dishes**: Butter chicken, seekh kebab, aloo chaat, nihari
- **Technique**: Tandoor mastery, layered parathas, slow braising

## Base Techniques

### 1. Tadka/Tempering
- Heat oil/ghee → add whole spices in sequence (hard spices first)
- Let aromatics bloom 5-15 seconds
- Pour over finished dal or start gravy with it
- Key: Don't burn; spices should darken and perfume

### 2. Onion-Tomato Gravy Base
- **Step 1**: Fry onions until deep golden (caramelization = sweetness)
- **Step 2**: Add ginger-garlic paste, cook raw smell out
- **Step 3**: Add tomatoes + powdered spices, cook until oil separates
- **Variations**: White (no tomato), onion-heavy, tomato-heavy

### 3. Yogurt Marinades
- Whisk yogurt smooth (no lumps)
- Add spices, ginger-garlic, lemon
- Marinate meat 4-24 hours
- Tenderizes + flavors deeply
- Used for: Tandoor items, kormas, biryanis

### 4. Bhunao (Sauté-Roasting)
- Cook onions/tomatoes/spices until oil separates
- Key sign: Ghee/oil pools around edges
- Concentrates flavors, removes raw taste
- Patience essential — 15-30 minutes common

### 5. Dum Pukht (Slow Steam)
- Seal pot with dough or tight lid
- Low heat, trapped steam cooks
- Flavors meld, meat tenderizes
- Used for: Biryani, korma, nihari

### 6. Tandoor Cooking
- Clay oven, charcoal-fired, 480°C+
- Skewered meats, naan slapped on walls
- Charred exterior, juicy interior
- Smoky signature flavor

## Essential Ingredients

### Dairy
- **Ghee**: Clarified butter, cooking medium, flavor base
- **Paneer**: Fresh cheese, non-melting, high protein
- **Yogurt (dahi)**: Marinades, raita, tenderizer
- **Cream/Malai**: Finishing rich dishes
- **Buttermilk (chaas)**: Cooling, Rajasthani cooking

### Proteins
- **Chicken**: Bone-in preferred for curries
- **Lamb/Goat**: Traditional, longer cooking
- **Paneer**: Vegetarian staple
- **Lentils (dal)**: Staple protein, multiple varieties

### Grains & Flours
- **Basmati rice**: Aromatic, long-grain, biryanis
- **Whole wheat (atta)**: Chapatis, parathas
- **Maida**: Naan, refined bread
- **Besan (gram flour)**: Batter, thickener, Rajasthani dishes

### Produce
- **Onions**: Red preferred, gravy base
- **Tomatoes**: Ripe, red, fresh or canned
- **Ginger-garlic**: Fresh paste, indispensable
- **Green chilies**: Heat (adjustable per taste)
- **Cilantro**: Fresh garnish
- **Mint**: Chutneys, biryanis
- **Fenugreek leaves (methi)**: Bitter, aromatic
- **Mustard greens (sarson)**: Punjab winter staple

### Legumes
- **Red lentils (masoor)**: Quick cooking
- **Yellow lentils (toor/arhar)**: Sambar, dal
- **Split chickpeas (chana dal)**: Sweet, nutty
- **Black lentils (urad)**: Dal makhani
- **Kidney beans (rajma)**: Punjabi staple
- **Chickpeas (chana)**: Chole, sundal

## Cooking Principles

1. **Spice layering**: Whole → ground → fresh (ginger/garlic/cilantro)
2. **Heat control**: High for tempering, medium for bhunao, low for dum
3. **Oil separation**: Sign of properly cooked gravy base
4. **Freshness matters**: Ginger-garlic paste daily, spices < 6 months
5. **Regional respect**: Don't mix techniques arbitrarily
6. **Acidity balance**: Tomatoes, amchur, yogurt brighten heavy dishes
7. **Garnish intentionally**: Fresh herbs, cream, fried onions add layers
